Meet the Team

Katharine Chin is a Taiwanese American actor and writer based in upstate New York. Her acting include: FBI (CBS), A Place in the Dark (Independent Feature), Not Him (short), Hot Idiot (short). Katharine has recorded numerous audiobooks with Harper Collins, Macmillan, & more. She is currently developing her next short film about a new mother who gets in the car, alone, and just starts driving. And driving. And driving. When she’s not busy making theatre or movies, you'll find her knitting, listening to audiobooks, and goofing off with her husband and two daughters.

Tori Ernst is an actor, producer and writer based in NYC. She grew up in Harrisburg PA, fly fishing with her dad on weekends and religiously listening to the entire David Bowie discography with my mom. She's half Greek and finds her Greek heritage has informed how she tells stories (lots of arm gestures and drama). Tori moved to NYC to attend Marymount Manhattan College where she earned her BFA. After graduation, Tori worked in off-off broadway theater for a few years. In 2012, she was a part of Theatermakers at the Eugene O’Neill Center NTI, where she discovered that I feel most inspired developing new works. Tori craves the collaborative process between actor, writer and director. In 2019 she graduated from The New School of Drama with my MFA in Acting where originated several roles including “Pearl” in Pony Up by Mallory Jane Weiss and “Andie” in The Tall Ones by Miles Orduña. In 2020, Tori wrote, produced and starred-in a short film called Counting, which centers on the life of a young woman struggling with severe OCD, based on Tori's own experience with OCD. The film went on to win several awards, was re-scored by Sony Music and was featured at the Cannes Film Festival 2022, Short Film Corner. Tori is passionate about telling complicated stories with curiosity and empathy. www.toriernst.com

Christopher F. Costa is a proud queer Brazilian-American born and raised in Miami, Florida. After working regionally in theaters throughout South Florida, Costa moved to New York City to study acting professionally. He trained at the Actors Movement Studio and also studied the Meisner Technique under JoAnna Beckson. After a few years of working professionally in theatre, TV, and film, Costa returned to school to pursue an MFA in Acting from The New School for Drama. He’s worked in TV on USA, NBC, and CBS, as well as in voiceover, including his new audiobook narration of Live to See the Day by Nikhil Goyal. Most recently, Costa is honored to have joined the Sesame Street family, especially as part of the GLAAD Media Award-winning team of “Family Day.” Costa plans to continue working on projects and collaborating with artists who value compassion, diversity, and inclusion, as well as being an advocate for queer youth. ChristopherFCosta.com.
